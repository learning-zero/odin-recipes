Cinnamon Rolls

Description

Cinnamon rolls are a beloved sweet treat known for their soft, fluffy texture and sweet, spicy filling. They typically consist of a yeast-based dough rolled with a mixture of cinnamon and sugar, then baked until golden brown. Often topped with a glaze or cream cheese frosting, cinnamon rolls are enjoyed as a breakfast pastry or a sweet snack. Their popularity spans across cultures, with variations found in many European countries, including Sweden, where they are known as kanelbulle, and Germany, where they are called zimtschnecken.

The origins of cinnamon rolls are tied to the availability of cinnamon in Europe during the 17th century. As cinnamon became more accessible, bakers began incorporating it into their recipes, leading to the development of early versions of the cinnamon roll. German and Swedish immigrants brought their own variations of these sweet pastries to America, where they evolved into the cinnamon rolls we know today. The modern American cinnamon roll gained widespread popularity with the rise of chains like Cinnabon in the late 20th century. Despite their evolution, cinnamon rolls remain a staple in many cultures, celebrated for their rich flavor and comforting warmth

Ingredients

Dough

  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup water at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg, room temperature
  • 4 cups bread flour
  • 1/2 (3.4 ounce) package instant vanilla pudding mix
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast

Filling

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup chopped pecans (Optional)
  • 4 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 cups warm milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened

Frosting

  • 1 cup confectioner's sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, softened
  • 1 1/2 taspoons milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Make the dough: Place milk, water, melted butter, egg, bread flour, pudding mix, sugar, salt, and yeast into a bread machine in the order listed, or follow the order recommended by the manufacturer if different. Run Dough cycle. Remove dough from the machine after the cycle is done, about 90 minutes.
  2. When the dough is almost finished, start the filling: Mix brown sugar, pecans, and cinnamon together in a bowl until well combined. Butter a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  3. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and roll into a 17x10-inch rectangle. Spread 1/2 cup softened butter over the rectangle, then sprinkle brown sugar mixture over top.
  4. Beginning with the long side, roll dough over filling into a log; cut log into sixteen 1-inch slices. Place slices, cut-sides up, into the prepared pan. Let rise in a warm place until doubled in volume, about 45 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  6. Bake rolls in the preheated oven until golden brown, 15 to 20 minutes. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes.
  7. While the rolls are cooling, make the frosting: Stir confectioners' sugar, softened butter, cream cheese, milk, and vanilla together in a bowl until well combined. Spread over warm rolls.
