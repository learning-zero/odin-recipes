Chocolate Chip Muffins

Description

Chocolate chip muffins are a beloved variation of the classic muffin, known for their rich, sweet flavor and the delightful surprise of melted chocolate chips within. They are made with a similar quick bread batter as other muffins but include the addition of chocolate chips, which add both flavor and texture. The combination of the moist, slightly dense muffin and the gooey chocolate creates a treat that is both comforting and indulgent. Chocolate chip muffins are often enjoyed as a breakfast item, snack, or dessert, and they are a staple in many bakeries and cafes.

The origins of chocolate chip muffins are closely tied to the invention of chocolate chips themselves. Ruth Wakefield, an American chef, is credited with accidentally inventing chocolate chip cookies in 1937 when she added chopped bits of chocolate to her cookie dough, expecting them to melt and distribute evenly. However, the chocolate retained its shape, creating a delightful surprise. This innovation soon influenced other baked goods, including muffins. The addition of chocolate chips to muffin batter became popular in the mid-20th century, as it provided a convenient way to incorporate chocolate into a quick and easy-to-make breakfast or snack item. Today, chocolate chip muffins remain a favorite among both children and adults, offering a sweet treat that combines the warmth of a freshly baked muffin with the indulgence of chocolate.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1 larg egg
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup mini semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Grease a 12-cup muffin tin or line cups with paper liners.

  3. Combine flour, 1/2 cup sugar, baking powder, and salt together in a large bowl, making a well in the center.
  4. Pour milk mixture into well and stir until batter is just combined; fold in chocolate chips.
  5. Spoon batter into the prepared muffin cups, filling each 2/3 full.
  6. Combine 1 Â½ tablespoons white sugar and 1 tablespoon brown sugar in a small bowl; sprinkle on tops of muffins.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until tops spring back when lightly pressed, about 18 to 20 minutes.
  8. Cool in the tin briefly, then transfer to a wire rack. Serve warm or cool completely.
