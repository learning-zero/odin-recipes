French Toast

Description

French toast is a beloved breakfast dish made by dipping slices of bread in a mixture of eggs, milk, and often spices like cinnamon, before frying them in a pan until golden brown. It is typically served with sweet toppings such as maple syrup, butter, and powdered sugar, though savory versions exist as well. The dish has a long history, with early versions dating back to ancient Rome, where a similar dish called "Pan Dulcis" was enjoyed. In France, it is known as "pain perdu," meaning "lost bread," because it was originally made with stale bread to prevent waste.

The name "French toast" was first used in 17th-century England, and the recipe was brought to America by early settlers. Over time, French toast has evolved with various cultural interpretations. In the United States, it is often served with sweet toppings, while in other countries, it may be served differently, such as with ketchup in the UK or bananas and bacon in New Zealand. French toast remains a versatile and popular dish, celebrated for its ability to transform stale bread into a delicious treat

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cups milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (Optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (Optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices of breadr
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  4. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  5. Serve hot.
